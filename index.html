Day 1
Master the 2-Minute Exit Ticket
The Why
Exit tickets help you quickly assess student understanding and identify who needs extra support before the next lesson. This simple strategy prevents students from falling behind and gives you actionable data in just 2 minutes.
The How
  1. In the last 5 minutes of class, ask students one specific question about today's lesson
  2. Have students write their answer on a small piece of paper with their name
  3. Collect the papers as students leave (this is your "exit ticket")
  4. Quickly sort responses into "got it," "almost there," and "needs help" piles
The Example
After teaching fractions, ask: "Draw a circle and shade 3/4 of it." Students who can do this accurately "got it," those who shade close to 3/4 are "almost there," and those who struggle need tomorrow's review group.
The Reflect

Think about it:

• What's one topic your students struggled with recently that an exit ticket could have caught earlier?

• How might knowing who "got it" vs. "needs help" change how you start tomorrow's lesson?

The Share

Try one exit ticket this week, then share with a colleague: "Here's the most surprising thing I learned about my students' understanding."
